What is a Step-Up SIP?

A Step-Up SIP (also called Top-Up SIP or Increasing SIP) is a systematic investment plan where you increase your monthly contribution by a fixed percentage every year — typically in line with your salary hike.

For example: Starting with ₹10,000/month and increasing by 10% every year means:

  • Year 1: ₹10,000/month
  • Year 2: ₹11,000/month
  • Year 3: ₹12,100/month
  • Year 4: ₹13,310/month
  • Year 10: ₹23,579/month

✅ Key insight: As your salary grows, your lifestyle costs grow too — but your SIP can grow faster if you commit to the step-up rule early.

How to Set Up Step-Up SIP in India

Method 1: Direct with AMC (Mutual Fund House)

Most AMCs (HDFC MF, SBI MF, Mirae, Axis etc.) allow you to set up a step-up SIP directly. During SIP registration, select "Step-Up" option and enter your annual increment percentage.

Method 2: Via Investment Apps

  • Groww: Go to SIP → select fund → enable "Top-up SIP" → set annual increment
  • Zerodha Coin: Supports step-up SIP during order placement
  • Kuvera: Has "Smart SIP" with step-up feature
  • Paytm Money: Step-up available on most equity funds

Method 3: Manual Annual Increase

Simply cancel your existing SIP every year and start a new one with a higher amount. More effort but gives you full control over the increment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal step-up percentage for SIP?
Financial advisors typically recommend matching your step-up percentage to your annual salary hike — usually 8–15%. A 10% annual step-up is the most commonly recommended as it balances growth with lifestyle sustainability.
Is step-up SIP better than flat SIP?
Yes, almost always. Step-up SIP generates significantly higher corpus while keeping early EMIs manageable. The compound effect of increasing investments year-on-year is very powerful over 15–20 year horizons.
Can I change the step-up amount later?
Yes. You can modify or cancel a step-up SIP at any time. Most platforms allow you to change the increment percentage or switch to a flat SIP with minimal notice.
Does step-up SIP affect LTCG tax?
Yes — higher corpus means potentially higher LTCG tax. However, the post-tax returns of a step-up SIP still significantly outperform a flat SIP. Use our calculator to see both the nominal and post-tax corpus of your step-up SIP.
